About Me

I'm Aletheia

I was born and raised in Turkey. I noticed my huge interest in design when I was a kid. I followed the path of my dreams and got into design school, graduated as a Textile Designer / Instructor.

I worked on fashion design in private companies for 10 years. I made paper patterns for famous brands, like H&M, Dolce Junior. Besides this, I worked as a teacher for 10 years, too, on design and artistic skills.

While I was living in Greece I worked as a freelancer designer using illustrator program for a textile company.

My Expertise

Fashion Design

Life Cycle

The name of the Project is Life Cycle. The Project represent the cycle from birth to death. I want to explain this awareness with an hourglass using LED lights in the cycle of the beginning of the universe – just like an hourglass in the life we ​​live. While we start the day calmly, we experience our hustle and bustle during the day, the work we need to do, the jobs that we need to do, while these continue, the clock continues to flow without any change and completes the 24 hours. I want to add the music suitable for the hourglass with led lights and colors. It will work with 12 different colors. The sunrise will start with yellow, orange, red and other colors will follow.

Hope for Refugees Kids

Hope for refugee children. Hundreds of thousands of children are forced to live in camps every year. Let there be hope for the children who have to live here. In my design, I will use seven colors, which symbolize the human chakras, with led lights. Children's energy is higher, their pulse and pulse work faster. I think the chakras are active. Also, all chakras are active in children due to energy, so you can captured them on the dress you designed with lights, and when they move, the lights start to turn on. Also, the pattern on the garment. The inspiration of the Project came from one of the object from the Collectie van De Genteenaar which is a motif from wooden door. This way I want to bring history to the present – door to hope. I also use the Reuseit concept when work with this Project.
